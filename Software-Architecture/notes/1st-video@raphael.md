## Session 1 - Video recording with Raphael

- Definition and Scope
  - What is Software Architecture?

    Software architecture is the high-level structure and design of a software system.

    It defines:
    1. The major components/services/modules
    2. How they interact
    3. Key technology choices
    4. Data flow
    5. Deployment structure
    6. Non-functional characteristics like scalability, security, and reliability

    It acts as the system’s “blueprint.”

  - Why do we do it?
    1. Manage system complexity
    2. Align technology with business goals
    3. Improve maintainability and scalability
    4. Reduce technical and project risk
    5. Enable teams to work independently
    6. Ensure quality attributes are met (performance, security, availability, etc.)
    7. Make long-term evolution easier
       Bottom line Good architecture helps avoid expensive redesign later.

- The role of a Software Architect

  ### what they do:

  A Software Architect:

  Defines system structure and boundaries
  Makes high-impact technical decisions
  Chooses architectural patterns and technologies
  Balances trade-offs
  Aligns engineering with business needs
  Guides teams technically
  Reviews designs and code at a high level
  Ensures quality attributes are achieved
  Facilitates communication between stakeholders
  Manages technical risks

  ### What they don't do
  1. Dictate every implementation detail
  2. Become a bottleneck
  3. Ignore business priorities
  4. Work in isolation from developers
  5. Replace engineering managers or product owners
  6. Write all the code themselves
